Paolo Fazzini received his M.Sc. in Nuclear Engineering from the University of Bologna, Italy, in 1994, and his Ph.D. in Engineering in Computer Science from La Sapienza University of Rome, Italy, in 2025. After completing mandatory civil service, he worked as a software designer and developer from 1996 to 2000, specializing in various fields of engineering, including finite-state machines, back-end web programming, and telecommunications. From 2001 to 2016, he was employed as a senior research engineer at Imagination Technologies, where he contributed to industrial research programs focused on cutting-edge 2D and 3D graphics, color science, digital image and video processing, and machine learning. During his tenure, he authored international patents on these subjects. Since 2017, he has been collaborating with the National Research Council (CNR) on machine learning-related topics, such as Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning, Forecasting, and signal decomposition techniques.
